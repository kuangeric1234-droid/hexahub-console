1. The one-paragraph brief (read this first)
HexaHub is a business infrastructure platform at 7 Distribution Circuit, Huntingdale, Melbourne — part of the 22,000+ sqm Found Huntingdale precinct. We give brands physical space (warehouses, storage, showrooms, offices, office+warehouse combos from 31–438 sqm), built-in operational infrastructure (24/7 access, 3-phase power, NBN, loading zones, CCTV, The Hub lounge), and a built-in ecosystem of partners (EIZ Technology for supply chain, Digitec IT, Australia Post logistics, plus mentorship). Our primary audiences are cross-border e-commerce brands entering Australia and local brands with fragmented operations. We are not a co-working space, not a traditional industrial lease, and not a logistics 3PL — we are the operating platform that sits across all of these. We are part of Hexa Group, alongside Hexa Space (Box Hill co-working) and our retail nodes at 369 Lonsdale St (CBD) and 878 Whitehorse Rd (Box Hill).
If an AI is ever uncertain whether something fits HexaHub, the test is: does it help a brand land, operate, or scale in Australia? If yes, it's on-brand. If it's about square metres or rent alone, it's off-brand.

4. Brand voice & tone
Voice principles

Operational, not aspirational. We talk about how things work, not how they feel. "3-phase power, 24/7 access, on-site Australia Post pickup" beats "elevate your business journey."
Confident, not loud. We don't shout, exclaim, or over-promise. No "!", no "REVOLUTIONARY," no "game-changing."
Plain English, low jargon. Our audience includes founders whose first language isn't English. Short sentences. Concrete nouns. If you must use jargon (3PL, fulfilment, EOI), define it once.
Show, don't tell. "Members get direct introductions to EIZ Technology and Australia Post" beats "we have an amazing partner network."
Outcome-driven. Frame benefits as what the member gets to do, not what the building has. "Help brands focus on growth" — not "facility features."

6. Audiences
6.1 Primary targets (highest priority)
A. Cross-border E-commerce brands entering Australia

Profile: Has supply chain (often China-based), wants to enter Australia, has no local operations base.
Pain: Strong supply chain but weak local setup. Needs an immediate operations base — warehouse, business address, Australia Post pickup, customs-ready paperwork — to start importing and shipping.
Hook angle: "Land faster. Operate sooner." Bilingual (EN/CN) creative converts strongly here.
Channels: WeChat, Xiaohongshu (small red book), LinkedIn, Chinese-language industry events, Hexa Group network.

B. Local Brands with Fragmented Operations

Profile: Already operating in Australia but warehouse, office, and logistics are in three different places.
Pain: High cost, low efficiency, coordination overhead. Needs consolidation onto one site without signing a 5-year industrial lease.
Hook angle: "Connected systems. Stronger operations." (consolidation story, cost-reduction angle).
Channels: LinkedIn, industry referrals, Google search (warehouse Melbourne, fulfilment space Melbourne), broker networks.

6.2 Growth-stage targets (secondary)
C. FMCG & Retail brands

Distribution-driven. Need storage and fulfilment capacity. Expansion-ready. Receptive to ecosystem partner story (EIZ logistics, Aus Post).

D. Startup & Emerging brands

Capital-sensitive. Need flexibility. Long-term growth potential. Storage spaces from $1,625/month and month-to-month terms are the right entry point.

6.3 Audiences we are NOT targeting

Residential renters / homebuyers
Retail-consumer end users
Pure office tenants with no logistics or product needs
Heavy-industrial manufacturing requiring >438 sqm or specialised compliance
Investors looking to buy units (we operate the platform, we don't sell stock)

16. Output rules for AI agents
When producing any HexaHub asset, the AI must:

Match audience to pillar. Pick one of the three selling-point pillars and stick with it.
Use approved language only. No invented slogans, taglines, or claims.
Stay inside the colour palette. No additional brand colours unless this document is updated.
Cite specifics over abstractions. Real partners (EIZ, Digitec IT, Australia Post), real sizes (31–438 sqm), real address (7 Distribution Circuit, Huntingdale).
Stay outcome-driven. "Help brands focus on growth" before "facility features."
Default to Australian English.
Don't fabricate pricing, member names, partner relationships, or stats. If a number isn't here or on the website, say "enquire."
Bilingual content needs a flag for review — output it, but tag for human translation check.
Always include a clear next step (Enquire, Book a Tour, Browse Spaces, link to hexahub.com.au/contact).
When in doubt, default to education before promotion.

Things AI agents should NEVER produce

Quotes attributed to William Yang, Chino Li, Leon Liu, or any member without explicit human approval.
Specific occupancy numbers, member counts, or revenue figures unless sourced from this doc or the website.
Prices not on the website.
Claims about other landlords, brokers, or named competitors that are negative or comparative without sign-off.
Health, legal, financial, or compliance advice — refer to partners and the team.
Misleading "limited time" / "only X units left" language unless the marketing team has confirmed inventory.
